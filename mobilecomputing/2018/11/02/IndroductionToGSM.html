Introduction to GSM

November-2-2018

Cellular Networks

  • wireless extensions of traditional PSTN(Public Switched Telephonic Networks) and ISDN(Integrated Services Digital Network) networks #telephones(wired).
  • mostly used for voice traffic
  • basic versions implement a circuit switched service
    • focused on voice
    • data - 9.6 kbits/s

Development of mobile communication technologies

  • First generation:
    • relied on FDMA
    • ex: CT0/1, AMPS, NMT
  • Second generation:
    • Added TDMA(used for channel seperation) to FDMA
    • AMPS evolved into D-AMPS
    • NMT added TDMA and evolved to GSM
  • Third Generation:
    • Second generation evolved over time and added CDMA later.
    • GRPS - introduced higher data rates for GSM
    • EDGE - proposed a new modulation scheme

GSM

  • Most successful digital mobile telecommunication system in the world today.
  • Groupe special mobile(GSM) - later renamed to Global System for Mobile Communication.
  • Primary Goal:

    Provide a mobile phone system that allows users to roam throughout europe & provide compatibility with ISDN & PSTN systems

  • Second Generation System was mainly designed for voice services
    • Intially deployed as GSM 900
      • 890 - 915 MHz for uplinks
      • 935 - 960 MHz for downlinks
    • Later as GSM 1800 - Digital Cellular System
      • 1710 - 1785 MHz uplinks
      • 1805 - 1880 MHz downlinks
    • In US GSM 1900 - Personal Communication Service
      • 1850 - 1910 MHz uplinks
      • 1930 - 1990 MHz downlinks
